# 9A Merged Memo

Packet: `9A`
Scope: `Section 9.3` only
PDF pages: `25-27`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. They agreed on the main structure:

- Theorem `9.1` is presented as an unconditional reduction theorem, but its
  proof is almost entirely citation-based and remains only `unclear` under the
  packet-level verification standard used in this project.
- Lemmas `9.2` and `9.3` are short internal arguments and both verify.
- Corollary `9.4` is not a proved corollary in the ordinary sense; it is an
  explicit formal reduction / remaining-input statement.
- Remark `9.5` is organizational and correctly says so.

## Normalized Claim

Theorem `9.1` claims that if `X` is a klt del Pezzo surface of Picard rank `1`
over an algebraically closed field of characteristic `3` with
`#Sing(X) >= 8`, then:

- `ht(X) >= 4`, and
- `X` has no descendant with elliptic boundary.

Equivalently, any hypothetical `8`-singularity counterexample already lies in
the residual high-height, no-elliptic-descendant sector.

Lemma `9.2` claims that if `L` is a nonexceptional `(-1)`-curve on the minimal
resolution with `L · D = 2`, then its image `C = pi(L)` on `X` is a nonzero
effective curve with `C^2 > 0`.

Lemma `9.3` claims that a singular klt del Pezzo surface of Picard rank `1`
cannot have height `0`.

Corollary `9.4` does not assert a proved existence theorem. It records the
remaining Route C entry statement that, in the residual sector, one should find a
contact-`2` `(-1)`-curve with root-contractible rooted configuration; by Lemma
`9.2`, any such configuration would automatically be positive.

Remark `9.5` explains that Theorem `9.1` is unconditional, Corollary `9.4` is
only a proof-program reduction, and the final proof later bypasses proving that
boxed residual entry statement separately.

## Agreed Findings

- The case split in Theorem `9.1` is logically correct once its three cited
  external exclusions are granted:
  height `<= 2`,
  height `= 3` with no elliptic-boundary descendant,
  and the elliptic-boundary descendant case.
- The local singularity counts cited inside Theorem `9.1` are numerically fine:
  `4 x [3] + 3A2` gives `7` singular points, and
  `4A2 + [5] + [3] + [2]` also gives `7`.
- What is not proved in-packet is that the cited classifications and listed
  primitive types really suffice to bound all surfaces in the relevant cases.
- The first two citation blocks in Theorem `9.1` are reasonably specific:
  `[7, Theorem A]` plus `[7, Remark 1.7]`,
  and `[8, Theorem A and Tables 4-7]` (with Table `5` being the characteristic
  `3` table actually relevant to the count).
- The last citation in Theorem `9.1` is unstable or mispointed as written:
  the packet cites `[7, Lemma 6.9(4)]`, but the matching four-type list appears
  as `[7, Lemma 6.21(4)]` in the checked arXiv version used by one reviewer.
- The final elliptic-descendant exclusion in Theorem `9.1` therefore compresses
  too much: it quotes a classification, quotes a primitive-type list, and jumps
  to `#Sing(X) <= 7` without spelling out the bridge from primitive types to all
  descendants.
- Lemma `9.2` verifies. Its proof uses only:
  klt implies `Q`-factorial,
  Picard rank `1`,
  and positivity of the square of an ample class.
  The hypotheses `L^2 = -1` and `L · D = 2` are not used in the written proof.
- Lemma `9.3` verifies once the imported definition of `ht(X) = 0` is granted.
  The inference `F · D = 0 => F ∩ D = ∅` is valid because `D` is effective and
  a general fiber meets each component nonnegatively.
- Corollary `9.4` is internally coherent as a conditional reduction, but it does
  not prove the boxed residual entry statement.
- Remark `9.5` accurately reclassifies Corollary `9.4` as programmatic rather
  than established.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Section `9.3` imports the meanings of:
  `ht(X)`,
  witnessing `P^1`-fibration,
  descendant with elliptic boundary,
  rooted configuration,
  root-contractible,
  positive,
  and benchmark counterexample.
- Theorem `9.1` depends almost entirely on outside classifications; the packet
  itself does not reproduce the relevant tables or classification arguments.
- In the third branch of Theorem `9.1`, the proof silently relies on the earlier
  elimination of the `ht(X) <= 2` case before invoking the elliptic-boundary
  classification.
- The citation to `[8, Tables 4-7]` is broader than the exact characteristic-`3`
  count actually used.
- Corollary `9.4` uses Lemma `9.2` to infer "positivity," but the precise bridge
  from `pi(L)^2 > 0` to positivity of a rooted configuration is imported from
  earlier definitions.

## Merged Verdict

`9A` is merged with a mixed positive-support finding:

- Theorem `9.1` is `unclear`:
  the reduction strategy is intelligible and the local counts are correct, but
  the argument is heavily citation-dependent and its final elliptic-descendant
  exclusion contains a citation mismatch plus a compressed inference.
- Lemma `9.2` is `verified`.
- Lemma `9.3` is `verified`, modulo the imported definition of height `0`.
- Corollary `9.4` is an organizational / formal reduction statement, not a
  proved corollary in this packet.
- Remark `9.5` is an organizational remark, not a theorem/proof item.
