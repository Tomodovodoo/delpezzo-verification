# 9B Merged Memo

Packet: `9B`
Scope: `Section 9.4` only
PDF pages: `27-28`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. All three agreed on the central conclusion:

- Lemma `9.6` has a real proof defect, not just a citation gap.
- Proposition `9.7` inherits that defect because its root-contractibility claim
  is built directly on Lemma `9.6`.
- Corollary `9.8` and Remark `9.9` are programmatic reductions / endgame notes,
  not independent proof closures.

## Normalized Claim

Lemma `9.6` claims that for a singular fiber `F_0` of a `P^1`-fibration and any
chosen multiplicity-one component `R ⊂ F_0`, there is a birational morphism to a
smooth `P^1`-bundle that contracts every component of `(F_0)_red - R` and sends
`R` to a smooth fiber.

Proposition `9.7` claims that if a vertical contact-`2` `(-1)`-curve `L` sits in
a connected component `T` of `D + L` whose components are all vertical and which
contains a multiplicity-one component of the ambient singular fiber, then the
associated rooted configuration is root-contractible; if `rho(X) = 1`, it is
also positive.

Corollary `9.8` records a stronger geometric statement that would suffice to
prove the residual Route C entry reduction from Corollary `9.4`: it would be
enough to produce such a purely vertical bridge with a multiplicity-one component
in the same connected vertical cluster.

Remark `9.9` explains what this would change in the endgame: it would remove the
need to check root-contractibility separately in the purely vertical case, while
still not proving Route C entry itself.

## Agreed Findings

- Lemma `9.6` appeals to standard ruled-surface / relative-minimal-model
  material, but the proof's decisive inference is not established:

  "because `R` has multiplicity `1`, its image on the relative minimal model is
  not a point."

- That statement is too strong as written. In elementary examples obtained by a
  single blowup of a smooth fiber, both components of the resulting singular
  fiber can have multiplicity `1`, yet a relative minimalization can contract
  either one.
- What the proof would need is an existence statement for a contraction sequence
  that preserves the chosen `R`, not merely an appeal to "the" relative minimal
  model.
- Since Lemma `9.6` does not establish that preservation claim, its conclusion is
  not verified.
- Proposition `9.7` is locally correct up to the point where it invokes
  Lemma `9.6`:
  from connectedness and verticality one indeed gets `T ⊂ F_0`, and the final
  positivity clause follows cleanly from Lemma `9.2` when `rho(X) = 1`.
- But the nontrivial root-contractibility step in Proposition `9.7` depends
  entirely on Lemma `9.6`, so Proposition `9.7` inherits the same gap.
- One reviewer noted an additional implicit point in Proposition `9.7`: the
  chosen multiplicity-one component must serve as a permissible root of the
  rooted configuration. The packet does not restate that definition, though the
  situation likely forces the multiplicity-one component to lie in the `D`-part
  rather than be `L` itself.
- Corollary `9.8` is logically fine as a formal implication if Proposition `9.7`
  is granted, but it is not an independent proof of the stronger vertical
  existence statement.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Lemma `9.6` uses uncited standard facts about:
  local structure of singular fibers of `P^1`-fibrations,
  existence of relative minimal models,
  and the fact that the relative minimal model is a smooth `P^1`-bundle.
- The packet does not distinguish between:
  1. existence of some relative minimalization, and
  2. existence of a relative minimalization preserving a specified component `R`.
  The latter is what the proof actually needs.
- Proposition `9.7` uses the notions "associated rooted configuration" and
  "root-contractible" without restating their formal definitions in this packet.
- The title of Proposition `9.7` and the prose of Remark `9.9` are broader than
  the formal statement: the criterion is not pure verticality alone, but
  pure verticality plus the multiplicity-one-component hypothesis.

## Merged Verdict

`9B` is merged with a negative-support finding:

- Lemma `9.6` is a `gap`, because its proof does not justify the claim that an
  arbitrary chosen multiplicity-one component survives to the final smooth fiber.
- Proposition `9.7` is a `gap`, because its root-contractibility step relies
  entirely on Lemma `9.6`.
- Corollary `9.8` is a formal reduction, not a proved theorem, and is only
  conditionally sound if Proposition `9.7` is accepted.
- Remark `9.9` is organizational/programmatic, not a theorem/proof item.
