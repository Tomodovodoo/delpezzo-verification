# 9C Merged Memo

Packet: `9C`
Scope: `Section 9.5a` only
PDF pages: `28-31`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. Two substantial points converged immediately:

- Proposition `9.12` is the one theorem-like item in this packet that verifies,
  modulo an imprecise citation tag.
- Corollary `9.13` contains a real local counting defect in part `(2)`, and its
  later conclusions depend on that part.

One major disagreement arose on Proposition `9.10`: one primary was willing to
accept it, while two judged it a gap. This was escalated to two neutral
`unsure` agents. Both agreed that Proposition `9.10` should be marked `gap`,
because:

- the cited `[7, Lemma 2.8(b)]` is stated for Picard-rank-one surfaces, while
  Proposition `9.10` does not assume `rho(X) = 1`, and
- the root-contractibility argument does not show that the full connected
  component `T` of `D + L` has unique horizontal root or that every non-root
  component of `T` is contracted by the relative-minimalization step.

## Normalized Claim

Proposition `9.10` claims that a multiplicity-one nonexceptional fiber component
`L` with exactly one horizontal contact and unique reduced-fiber neighbor in `D`
already gives a contact-`2`, root-contractible configuration, and is positive in
Picard rank `1`.

Corollary `9.11` records the corresponding stronger mixed sufficient condition:
to prove the formal residual Route C entry statement, it would suffice to
produce such an `L` on a witnessing `P^1`-fibration in the residual sector.

Proposition `9.12` claims that on the minimal resolution of a singular klt del
Pezzo surface, no fiber of any `P^1`-fibration is contained in the exceptional
divisor `D`; equivalently `nu_infty = 0`.

Corollary `9.13` claims that in the height-`4`, maximal-width setting:

- `Sigma = sum_F (sigma(F) - 1) = 3`,
- no multiplicity-one nonexceptional component can meet all four horizontal
  sections,
- the bad singly-hit set `B1` has size at most `3`,
- hence at most three fibers contain such components,
- and one horizontal `1`-section `H0` avoids all of them.

Remarks `9.14-9.15` explain the programmatic upshot of Corollary `9.13` and
compare it with the canonical `4A2` height-`4` example from `[6]`.

## Agreed Findings

- In Proposition `9.10`, the local count `L · D = 2` is better supported than
  the rest: the hypothesis already supplies one vertical neighbor in `D`, and
  `L · D_hor = 1` gives one horizontal contact.
- But the proposition's first claim, that `L` is a `(-1)`-curve, is justified in
  the packet only by `[7, Lemma 2.8(b)]`, whose stated scope in the checked
  source is Picard rank `1`. Proposition `9.10` does not assume that.
- More importantly, the root-contractibility step in Proposition `9.10` is not
  established. From the fact that `L` meets one horizontal component `H`, it
  does not follow that the whole connected component `T` of `D + L` has unique
  horizontal component `H`, nor that every component of `T - H` lies in the
  reducible fiber `F_0` and is contracted by relative minimalization.
- Corollary `9.11` is not an independent proof. It is a formal reduction that is
  only as strong as Proposition `9.10`.
- Proposition `9.12` verifies:
  if a fiber `F` were contained in `D`, then minimality would force every
  component of `F` to have self-intersection at most `-2`, so `F` would be
  degenerate; a degenerate fiber contains a `(-1)`-curve; that `(-1)`-curve
  would lie in `D`, contradicting minimality.
- The only issue with Proposition `9.12` is citation precision: the paper says
  "Lemma 2.3" without a clear source marker, and this appears to mean the
  standard external degenerate-fiber lemma `[7, Lemma 2.3]`.
- In Corollary `9.13`, the computation `Sigma = 3` is correct.
- Part `(1)` of Corollary `9.13` is also correctly reduced to `[7, Lemma 2.8(c)]`.
- The defect is in part `(2)`: after bounding `b(F)` by a sum of `(m_i - 1)`
  over the components with `m_i >= 2`, the displayed equality

  `sum_(m_i >= 2) (m_i - 1) = sigma(F) - #{i : m_i >= 2}`

  is false as written when singleton components `m_i = 1` are present.
- The desired final inequality `b(F) <= sigma(F) - 1` may be repairable by a
  slightly different argument or an explicit `b(F) = 0` case split, but the text
  does not provide that repair.
- Therefore part `(3)` of Corollary `9.13`, and the end-of-proof "Consequently"
  clause, inherit a real gap from part `(2)` and additionally depend on
  Proposition `9.10`.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Proposition `9.10` uses `[7, Lemma 2.8(b)]` outside its stated Picard-rank-one
  scope.
- Proposition `9.10` also uses the notion of root-contractibility without
  restating the formal definition in this packet.
- Proposition `9.12` cites "Lemma 2.3" imprecisely; the intended source appears
  to be `[7, Lemma 2.3]`.
- Corollary `9.13` uses `rho(tilde X) = rho(X) + #D`, which is standard for
  minimal resolutions of rational singularities but not re-explained here.
- Remarks `9.14-9.15` are organizational / comparative statements rather than
  new theorem-proof content.

## Discrepancy Resolution

The only major conflict in `9C` concerned Proposition `9.10`.

- One primary reviewer accepted the proposition, treating the cited external
  lemma and relative-minimalization argument as sufficient.
- Two primary reviewers judged that both the citation scope and the
  root-contractibility step were real flaws.

Two neutral `unsure` agents agreed with the stricter reading:

- the local contact count `L · D = 2` is the best-supported part of the
  proposition,
- but the packet does not justify the `(-1)`-curve claim via the cited lemma,
  and it does not prove the stronger root-contractibility conclusion,
- so Proposition `9.10` should be marked `gap`.

## Merged Verdict

`9C` is merged with a mixed negative-support finding:

- Proposition `9.10` is a `gap`.
- Corollary `9.11` is an organizational / formal reduction, not an independent
  theorem, and is only conditionally sound if Proposition `9.10` is accepted.
- Proposition `9.12` is `verified`, modulo an imprecise citation tag.
- Corollary `9.13` is a `gap` because part `(2)` contains a real counting error,
  and the later clean-section conclusion inherits that defect.
- Remarks `9.14-9.15` are organizational / comparative rather than proof items.
