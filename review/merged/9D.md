## Normalized Claim

Proposition `9.16` claims that after choosing a clean horizontal `1`-section
`H_0` and contracting all vertical curves disjoint from it, one obtains a
Hirzebruch surface `F_n` in which:

- `S_0 = nu(H_0)` is the negative section with `n = -H_0^2 >= 2`,
- every other horizontal `1`-section `H` maps to a section `S` with
  `S^2 = n + 2(H · H_0)` and class `S ~ S_0 + (n + H · H_0)f`,
- and the unresolved mixed maximal-width problem can be reformulated in terms of
  the three remaining sections `S_i = nu(H_i)` and at most three bad fibers.

Corollary `9.17` claims that when `H_0 · H_i = 0` for `i = 1,2,3`, the three
remaining sections all satisfy `S_i ~ S_0 + nf` and `S_i^2 = n`.

Proposition `9.18` claims that, under that disjointness assumption and the extra
hypothesis that all pairwise intersections of `S_1,S_2,S_3` are supported over
at most three fibers, the triple has one of two explicit normal forms after
base/ruling automorphisms:

- a scalar-multiple common-monomial model, or
- a Frobenius-monomial model of degree `3^e` in characteristic `3`.

Corollary `9.19` repackages Proposition `9.18` for the case where the pairwise
intersections are supported on the marked fibers coming from Corollary `9.13`.

## Agreed Findings

- In Proposition `9.16`, the contraction away from `H_0` is an isomorphism near
  `H_0`, so `S_0^2 = H_0^2 < 0`, and the identification of `S_0` as the unique
  negative section on `F_n` is standard.
- The bound `n = -H_0^2 >= 2` is justified once one imports the standard fact
  that an exceptional component on a minimal resolution is not a `(-1)`-curve.
- The numerical calculation in Proposition `9.16(2)` is correct:
  if `S ~ S_0 + bf`, then

  `0 = (S - S_0)^2 = S^2 - 2(H · H_0) - n,`

  and also `S · S_0 = b - n = H · H_0`, so
  `S^2 = n + 2(H · H_0)` and `b = n + H · H_0`.
- The special cases `H · H_0 = 0` and `H · H_0 = 1` follow immediately.
- Proposition `9.16(3)` is not a new geometric deduction so much as a
  reformulation: it packages the remaining mixed problem on `F_n` using the
  three sections `S_1,S_2,S_3` and the bad fibers from Corollary `9.13`.
- Corollary `9.17` is an immediate consequence of Proposition `9.16(2a)`.
- In Proposition `9.18`, the translation/scaling normalizations on
  `F_n - S_0 ~= Tot(O_{P^1}(n))` are mathematically coherent.
- The identification of pairwise intersections with the zero divisors of
  `q_2`, `q_3`, and `q_2 - q_3` is correct.
- The monomial-support reduction over three fibers is correct after moving the
  support to `{0,1,∞}` by a base automorphism.
- The gcd reduction and the relation `u - v = w` among pairwise coprime
  monomials are correct.
- The characteristic-`3` step is also correct:
  vanishing of all intermediate binomial coefficients forces `d = 3^e` by
  Lucas' theorem, producing the Frobenius normal form in case `(ii)`.
- Corollary `9.19` is immediate once its support hypothesis is granted.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- The packet repeatedly imports ambient setup from Corollary `9.13` and
  Proposition `9.16` without restating it.
- Proposition `9.16` assumes standard ruled-surface facts:
  the base is `P^1`,
  the relatively minimal ruled target is a Hirzebruch surface `F_n`,
  and exceptional curves on the minimal resolution are not `(-1)`-curves.
- Proposition `9.18` uses characteristic `3` essentially but does not restate
  that locally.
- Proposition `9.18` also uses the ambient algebraically closed setup to move
  the support set `T` to `{0,1,∞}`.
- Lucas' theorem is named but not bibliographically cited.
- Several lines use non-formal language such as "reformulated," "becomes a
  problem," and "encoded"; these should be read as proof-program packaging, not
  as uniqueness claims.

## Merged Verdict

`9D` is merged with a mostly positive-support finding:

- Proposition `9.16(1)-(2)` is `verified`.
- Proposition `9.16(3)` is best treated as an organizational reformulation whose
  geometric ingredients are supported.
- Corollary `9.17` is `verified`, with its final sentence again best read
  organizationally.
- Proposition `9.18` is `verified` as a conditional normal-form result.
- Corollary `9.19` is `verified` as an immediate conditional packaging statement,
  provided its support hypothesis is understood as an extra assumption rather
  than something proved here.
