## Normalized Claim

Lemma `9.20` resolves a two-section collision of order `p` into an explicit chain
`F_1 - ... - F_p`, ending in one terminal `(-1)`-curve.

Proposition `9.21` adds a common-factor stripping stage: after `u` common
blowups and then resolving the remaining primitive order-`p` collision, the total
exceptional divisor is the chain

`G_1 - ... - G_u - F_1 - ... - F_p`

with all intermediate self-intersections explicitly described.

Corollary `9.22` applies this to the Frobenius normal form from Proposition
`9.18(ii)`, showing that every marked Frobenius collision is locally of the form

`z = 0, z = t^u, z = t^(u+3^e)`

up to units and permutation.

Proposition `9.23` claims that in this explicit local chain, all curves
`G_1,...,G_u,F_1,...,F_{p-1}` belong to `D`, while the final curve `F_p` does
not.

Corollary `9.24` claims that `L := F_p` is the unique local nonexceptional
vertical `(-1)`-curve, and that the marked-collision core is either:

- a two-contact case when `u = 0, p = 1`, or
- a three-armed fork in every other case.

Proposition `9.28` gives the local same-component contact-`2` package:

- `p_a(D_0 + L) = 1`,
- `C^2 = -1 - v^t M^(-1) v > 0`,
- and explicit chain formulas imposing weight bounds such as `a_i <= 5` in the
  doubly-hit chain case.

## Agreed Findings

- Lemma `9.20` is internally complete. The recursive chart calculation
  `z = t^k w_k` is correct, the collision disappears exactly after `p` blowups,
  and the resulting chain/self-intersection data are tracked correctly.
- Proposition `9.21` is also internally complete. After `u` common blowups, the
  residual collision is exactly an order-`p` pair, and the extra drop
  `G_u^2 : -1 -> -2` when the primitive pair-resolution begins on `G_u` is
  correctly accounted for.
- Corollary `9.22` is a correct local application of Proposition `9.18(ii)` and
  Proposition `9.21`.
- Proposition `9.23` is sound once one accepts the cited external lemma
  `[7, Lemma 2.8(b)]`: the local self-intersection data from Proposition `9.21`
  and the fact that `D` contains no exceptional `(-1)`-curve force exactly the
  stated D-membership pattern.
- Corollary `9.24` is not fully proved as written.
  The special case `u = 0, p = 1` is handled correctly, but the general
  “every other case” argument repeatedly uses the existence of `F_(p-1)`.
  That fails in the subcase `u > 0, p = 1`, where the unique vertical neighbor
  of `L = F_1` is `G_u`, not `F_0` or `F_(p-1)`.
- So the intended conclusion `L · D = 3` may still be true in that subcase, but
  it is not established by the proof as written.
- Proposition `9.28` is mathematically solid.
  The arithmetic genus computation, orthogonality construction of `pi^* C`,
  formula

  `C^2 = -1 - v^t M^(-1) v`,

  and the explicit chain formulas all check out.
- In the chain case, the determinant expansion and the derived bound `a_i <= 5`
  are correct, and the extra neighbor constraint when `a_i = 5` is also
  correctly deduced.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Proposition `9.23` depends essentially on the cited external
  `[7, Lemma 2.8(b)]`; the packet cites it precisely enough, but does not
  re-prove it.
- Proposition `9.28` assumes earlier notation for chains and discriminants
  `d(T)` and cites the inverse formula for continued-fraction matrices only by
  description, though the displayed formula is explicit enough to check locally.
- Remark `9.29` states a fork formula

  `C^2 = -1 + 4 d(T_1) d(T_2) d(T_3) / d(D_0),`

  but provides no derivation beyond “the same Cramer's-rule computation.”
- Remarks `9.25-9.27` and `9.30`, and the opening paragraph of `Section 9.6`,
  are proof-program statements: they describe what the packet is supposed to
  reduce the global problem to, but they do not prove new theorem-level claims.

`9E` is merged with a mostly positive-support finding:
