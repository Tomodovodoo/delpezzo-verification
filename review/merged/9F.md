## Normalized Claim

Proposition `9.31` gives a first rooted-graph pass on the short frontier chains
`[2]`, `[3]`, `[5]`, `[6]`, `[2,2]`, `[3,2]`, `[4,2]`, `[5,2]`, separating the
positive contact-`2` patterns from the root-contractible ones.

Proposition `9.33` analyzes the three-vertex family
`T_k = [3,k,2]`, claiming that:

- the tip-to-tip pattern has
  `C^2 = (5-k)/(6k-5)`,
- positivity holds exactly for `k <= 4`,
- and for `k >= 3` no other positive contact-`2` pattern is
  root-contractible.

Proposition `9.34` claims a uniform classification on
`T_m = [3,(2)^m]`:

- the end-supported patterns `(T_1,T_2)` and `(T_1,T_{m+1})` are
  root-contractible with explicit positive squares,
- the one-vertex case `[3]|1,1|` and one short off-end pattern on
  `[3,2,2,2]` are the only further root-contractible cases,
- and therefore for `m >= 4` only the two end-supported patterns remain.

## Agreed Findings

- Proposition `9.31(1)-(3)` checks out as written.
- In Proposition `9.31(4)`, the claim that the only positive pattern on `[5,2]`
  is double contact on the `(-2)`-tip is not established by the cited
  Proposition `9.28(3)` alone, because the tip-to-tip pattern is a
  Proposition `9.28(4)` computation. But that missing step is immediate:
  the tip-to-tip pattern has `C^2 = 0`, while double contact on the `(-2)`-tip
  has `C^2 = 11/9 > 0`.
- The remaining part of Proposition `9.31(4)` is correct:
  that unique positive pattern is not root-contractible after blowing down `L`.
- Proposition `9.33` is internally stable. The determinant identities and the
  positivity formula are correct, the double-contact cases are excluded
  correctly, and the tip-to-tip rooted contraction works exactly as claimed.
- In Proposition `9.34`, parts `(1)` and `(2)` verify, and the short exceptional
  pattern on `[3,2,2,2]` in part `(3)` also verifies with `C^2 = 13/9`.
- The proof of Proposition `9.34(3)` does not correctly track blowdown
  multiplicities when the contacted components are consecutive in the chain.
  In those cases, blowing down `L` creates an extra edge on top of an existing
  edge, so later self-intersection jumps are by `m^2`, not by `1`.
- As a result, several printed impossibility steps are false as written. For
  example, in consecutive-contact subcases the proof repeatedly says that
  contracting one `(-1)`-curve makes another curve have self-intersection `0`,
  when the actual value can be positive.
- Since part `(4)` is declared immediate from part `(3)`, Proposition `9.34`
  is not fully established at packet level.
- Remark `9.32` is best read as synthesis rather than as a new theorem item.
  Its closing sentence about the “surviving local models” needs extra context:
  Proposition `9.31(3)` still leaves the tip-to-tip pattern on `[3,2]`
  root-contractible.
- Remark `9.35` is a programmatic summary/preview and inherits the uncertainty
  in Proposition `9.34`.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- The packet repeatedly uses standard blowdown formulas for self-intersections
  and intersections without citing them locally.
- Proposition `9.31(4)` under-cites the positivity check on `[5,2]`:
  Proposition `9.28(3)` handles the double-contact cases, but the tip-to-tip
  pattern needs Proposition `9.28(4)`.
- Proposition `9.34(3)` tacitly suppresses edge multiplicity created by
  blowdown in consecutive-contact subcases.
- Remark `9.32` forward-cites Lemma `9.41` for the bridge from
  “not root-contractible” to “not locally sandwiched of delta = 1.”
- External citations `[6, Proposition 6.1]` and `[7, Lemma 6.2]` are specific
  enough, but their content is not re-proved here.

## Merged Verdict

`9F` is merged with a mixed-support finding:

- Proposition `9.31` is `verified`, with a local citation/immediacy issue in
  part `(4)` but no substantive packet-level defect.
- Proposition `9.33` is `verified`.
- Proposition `9.34` is a `gap`, because its exclusion proof in part `(3)`
  mishandles double-edge blowdown updates, and part `(4)` depends on that
  argument.
- Remarks `9.32` and `9.35` are organizational/programmatic, not independent
  theorem closures.
