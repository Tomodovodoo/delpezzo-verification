## Normalized Claim

Lemma `9.36` records the standard monotonicity of self-intersection under
blowdown and uses it to freeze any non-root component that becomes nonnegative.

Proposition `9.37` studies the end-supported contact patterns on
`T_n(a) = [a,(2)^{n-1}]`, `a in {3,4}`, computing `C^2` explicitly and claiming
that root-contractibility occurs exactly for:

- `k = 2` or `k = n` when `a = 3`,
- `k = 2`, `k = n-1`, or `k = n` when `a = 4`.

Proposition `9.39` studies the off-end family where `L` meets `E_2` and `E_n`,
claiming:

- `C^2 = (an+1)/((a-1)n+1) > 0`,
- root-contractibility holds exactly when `n = a + 1`,
- and the two surviving cases are explicit elliptic ties.

Lemma `9.41` claims that for a connected reduced SNC divisor `E` with smooth
rational components and `p_a(E) = 1`, keeping a chosen component `R`
birationally is equivalent to a blowdown sequence contracting only curves from
the current image of `E - R` until only `R` remains. It then identifies this
with local sandwichedness of `delta = 1` for the rooted graphs under study.

Theorem `9.42` claims an exact local classification of the positive
contact-`2` patterns on `T_n(a)` that are locally sandwiched of `delta = 1`.

## Agreed Findings

- Lemma `9.36` is solid. The blowdown formula is correct, the monotonicity
  conclusion is immediate, and the frozen-component corollary is valid.
- Proposition `9.37` is mathematically stable. The discriminant computation
  `d([a,(2)^{n-1}]) = (a-1)n + 1` is correct, as are the displayed `C^2` formula
  and its specializations.
- The converse obstruction arguments in Proposition `9.37` also check: for
  `a = 3`, interior cases produce the expected triangle/frozen-component
  obstruction; for `a = 4`, the same monotonicity argument excludes all
  interior cases except the listed ones.
- The only conflict in Proposition `9.37` is presentational:
  in the `a = 4`, `k = n-1` branch, the middle displayed sequence degenerates
  badly when `n = 3`, because then `k = n-1 = 2` is the same case already
  covered by the first explicit sequence. The `unsure` reviewers agreed this is
  a boundary-case wording issue, not a packet-level proof gap.
- Proposition `9.39` is also stable. The square computation is correct, the
  forcing argument that every rooted contraction must begin
  `L, E_2, E_3, ..., E_{n-1}` is correct, and the condition
  `E_1^2 = -a + n - 2 = -1` gives the exact criterion `n = a + 1`.
- In the `n = 3` subcase of Proposition `9.39`, one local self-intersection
  sentence is imprecise: after blowing down `L`, the surviving adjacency can be
  double rather than simple. But the proof only needs the weaker conclusion that
  the other component becomes nonnegative, so the argument survives.
- The last sentence of Proposition `9.39`, upgrading the two surviving cases to
  “explicit elliptic ties,” is not re-proved inside the proposition. Still, the
  `unsure` reviewers agreed that packet-level it is supported by the paper’s
  already-used same-component contact-`2` setup: Proposition `9.28(1)` supplies
  arithmetic genus `1`, and rooted contractibility is already being used as a
  sufficient local tie witness in this corridor.
- Lemma `9.41` has a real proof problem in the direction `(1) => (2)`.
  From the statement that every component of `E - R` is `phi`-exceptional, the
  proof does not justify that one may choose a `(-1)`-curve among the current
  images of components of `E - R` at every stage. Extra `phi`-exceptional curves
  outside `E` may have to be contracted first.
- Lemma `9.41` also asserts
  `phi_* O_E = O_{phi(E)}` and `R^1 phi_* O_E = 0`
  without a precise theorem citation. The later genus computation is fine if
  those assertions are granted.
- Theorem `9.42` inherits the Lemma `9.41` gap wherever it uses
  “locally sandwiched of delta = 1 iff root-contractible.”
- In addition, the `a = 4` exclusion in Theorem `9.42` is not proved from the
  cited packet-level inputs. Proposition `9.37` handles only end-supported
  patterns, Proposition `9.39` handles one specific off-end family, and Remark
  `9.38` is only a finite search through chain length `20`, not a theorem
  excluding all remaining off-end patterns.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Proposition `9.37` has a boundary-case presentation defect in the
  `a = 4, k = n-1, n = 3` overlap, but not a substantive proof defect.
- Proposition `9.39` uses Proposition `9.28(1)` precisely for `p_a = 1`, but
  its final tie language relies on the already-established rooted-contractible
  witness principle rather than a fresh in-packet citation.
- Lemma `9.41` tacitly needs either:
  1. a stronger hypothesis ruling out extra `phi`-exceptional curves outside
     `E - R`, or
  2. an argument showing they can be bypassed in the factorization.
- Lemma `9.41` also uses direct-image/vanishing facts for
  `E -> phi(E)` without naming a theorem.
- Remark `9.38` is a finite-search report, not theorem-level support for a
  global exact classification.

## Merged Verdict

`9G` is merged with a mixed-support finding:

- Lemma `9.36` is `verified`.
- Proposition `9.37` is `verified`, with a small boundary-case presentation slip
  that does not affect the proposition’s actual classification.
- Proposition `9.39` is `verified`.
- Lemma `9.41` is a `gap`, because its proof of `(1) => (2)` does not justify
  choosing contraction curves inside the current image of `E - R` at every
  stage.
- Theorem `9.42` is a `gap`, both because it relies on Lemma `9.41` and because
  its `a = 4` exact exclusion uses only the finite-search Remark `9.38` rather
  than a theorem-level all-`n` argument.
