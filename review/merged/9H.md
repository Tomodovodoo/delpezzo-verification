## Normalized Claim

Proposition `9.44` claims an exact classification of the positive
root-contractible same-component contact-`2` patterns on the first fork frontier
`F_b = <b; [2], [3], [5]>`.

Proposition `9.46` gives the basic local package for distinct-component
bridges:

- `p_a(D_1 + D_2 + L) = 0`,
- `C^2 = -1 - v_1^t M_1^{-1} v_1 - v_2^t M_2^{-1} v_2 > 0`,

Proposition `9.48` claims a first exact chain-bridge classification from

Proposition `9.50` claims an exact distinct-component bridge classification
from `[2]` or `[2,2]` into the first fork frontier `F_b`.

## Agreed Findings

- Proposition `9.46` is solid. The genus-zero tree computation is correct, the
  square formula follows by orthogonality, and the chain specialization is the
  standard inverse-tridiagonal diagonal-entry formula.
- Proposition `9.47` is also solid. The linear-system computation for the
  coefficients of `Gamma_A`, the telescoping identity, and the pullback step
  from `tilde X` to `X` are all correct.
- Proposition `9.50` is mathematically stable. The inverse-matrix diagonal
  entries, positivity ranges, and case-by-case blowdown exclusions all check.
  The closing finite search over `2 <= b <= 200` is not the proof and is not
  needed for the conclusion.
- Proposition `9.44` contains a real defect in its exhaustion proof. In the
  single-contact `(B,U)` case, after contracting `L` the edge `BU` becomes
  double, so blowing down `U` changes `B^2` by `4`, not by `1`. The printed
  elimination therefore uses the wrong self-intersection update, and part `(3)`
  is not proved as written.
- Although the final conclusion of Proposition `9.44` may still be true, the
  packet does not correctly establish the claimed exhaustive classification.
- Proposition `9.48(1)` is fine: the determinant formula, positivity, and the
  displayed contraction sequence for the `[2]`-side root all check.
- Proposition `9.48(2)` is not correct as written unless the root is explicitly
  fixed on the `[2,2]`-side. The proof only analyzes sequences preserving `U_2`
  as root. But without that extra convention, the `a = 4` case is also
  root-contractible by contracting
  `L, U_1, U_2, E_1, ..., E_{n-1}`
  and leaving `E_n` as the root.
- Those extra `a = 4` cases have negative `C^2`, so they do not create new
  positive survivors. But the proposition’s unqualified “if and only if
  `a = 3`” is false as stated.
- Proposition `9.48(3)` is also only an undocumented finite search for
  `2 <= n <= 20`, with no packet-level proof certificate or extension to all
  `n`.
- Remarks `9.45`, `9.49`, and `9.51` summarize consequences and next steps but
  do not prove new theorem-level claims.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Proposition `9.44` ends by calling the surviving genus-one rooted graphs

- Propositions `9.46-9.47` invoke the standard inverse formula for tridiagonal

  statement, though redundant.
