## Normalized Claim

Proposition `9.52` claims that the infinite peelable family
`[2,2] ⊣ [3,(2)^{n-1}]` is a determinant-one staircase which contracts to a
smooth point while leaving a surviving rational curve `R_n` with
`R_n^2 = n - 1`.

Corollary `9.53` then reduces every member of that family, away from the rest
boundary, to a single prototype `[2,2] ⊣ [3,2]`.

Proposition `9.55` places a square-one rational curve on a birational plane
model as the line at infinity.

Proposition `9.56` equips the prototype with a distinguished ruling whose
special fiber contains the whole infinity cluster and whose later blowups occur
away from that fiber.

Theorem `9.58` claims that every positive root-contractible contact-`2`
configuration on the first analyzed chain/fork/cross-link frontier is exactly
one of:

- an elliptic tie,
- the peelable family `[2,2] ⊣ [3,(2)^m]`,
- or one of four isolated short bridges.

## Agreed Findings

- Proposition `9.52` is numerically strong. The determinant calculation
  `d(S_n) = 1` is correct, the displayed contraction sequence is correct, and
  the self-intersection computation `R_n^2 = -2 + 1 + n = n - 1` is correct.
- One sentence in Proposition `9.52(3)` is false as written: the contracted
  curves are not disjoint from the smooth locus of the current transform of
  `U_2`. But the `unsure` reviewers agreed this does not create a packet-level
  gap, because the displayed contraction order still shows a sequence of
  blowdowns meeting the current transform transversely at one smooth point each,
  so the image remains a smooth rational curve.
- Corollary `9.53` verifies. The preliminary `n - 2` blowups reduce the square
  to `1`, preserve boundary disjointness, and the four-step cluster creates the
  stated prototype chain.
- Proposition `9.55` verifies. The cohomology count, base-point-freeness,
  birationality argument, and “contracted curves are disjoint from `R`” step all
  check.
- Proposition `9.56` verifies. The ruling after the first blowup at `p`, the
  section property of `E_2`, the special fiber support, and the persistence
  under later blowups over `A^2` are all correct.
- Theorem `9.58` has a real dependency-level failure. In the terminal-tail
  bridge case it says Proposition `9.48` gives the complete list of positive
  root-contractible cross-links, but Proposition `9.48(3)` only reports a
  finite search for `2 <= n <= 20`. No argument upgrades that finite search to
  an all-`n` exhaustion.
- Because `9I` depends on earlier packets, Theorem `9.58` also sits downstream
  of already unresolved items, especially Proposition `9.44`, Proposition
  `9.48`, Lemma `9.41`, and Theorem `9.42`. So even apart from its own citation
  overreach, it is not supported by a fully verified dependency spine.
- Remarks `9.54` and `9.57` are best read as roadmap summaries, not proof items.
