## Normalized Claim

Proposition `9.61` claims that each of the four short bridges from Theorem
`9.58(iii)` peels to a surviving positive root on a smooth rational surface,
with root squares `5, 11, 1, 1`.

Proposition `9.62` then claims that each of those short bridges admits an
explicit global line-at-infinity model on `(P^2, L∞)`, directly in the fork
cases and after `4` or `10` auxiliary blowups in the two tail cases.

Theorem `9.63` packages the earlier local reductions and says that, assuming
the boxed Route C entry statement and frontier completeness, every
many-singularity surface reduces to one of five explicit global prototypes.

Proposition `9.65` claims that the two fork prototypes `[2] -> F_3` and
`[2,2] -> F_4` force descendants with elliptic boundary by identifying the
post-contraction fork `<1;[2],[3],[5]>` with an external cuspidal elliptic
boundary model.

Proposition `9.68` claims that the square-one peelable prototype carries a
distinguished ruling whose general fiber meets the relevant boundary once.

## Agreed Findings

- Proposition `9.61` is locally strong. All three primaries agreed that the
  contraction arithmetic is correct:
  `[2] ⊣ [3,2,2]` gives root squares `-2,-1,0,1,5`,
  `[2] ⊣ [4,2,2,2]` gives `-2,-1,0,1,2,11`,
  `[2] -> F_3` gives `-5,-5,-5,-4,-3,1`,
  and `[2,2] -> F_4` ends at `1`.
- The packet-level disagreement in Proposition `9.61` is not about the local
  calculations. It is about whether the proof adequately establishes the
  statement's use of a smooth rational target surface `Y`. Two primaries treat
  this as a standard/citation-level issue; one primary treats it as a real gap.
  Because the `unsure` adjudications failed to return usable reports, this memo
  resolves Proposition `9.61` conservatively as `unclear`.
- Proposition `9.62` is formally plausible: the square reductions `5 -> 1` and
  `11 -> 1` are correct, and the inverse-cluster picture over one point of
  `L∞` is consistent with the stated contraction sequences. But its use of
  Proposition `9.55` depends on whether Proposition `9.61` really furnishes the
  required smooth rational output. So Proposition `9.62` remains `unclear`.
- Theorem `9.63` is a formal splice of Corollary `9.59`, Corollary `9.53`,
  Propositions `9.55-9.56`, and Proposition `9.62`. No new calculation fails
  here, but its short-bridge branch inherits the Proposition `9.62`
  uncertainty, so the theorem remains `unclear`.
- Proposition `9.65` has verified local contractions: both fork cases contract
  to `<1;[2],[3],[5]>`. The packet's remaining dispute is whether the external
  citation to `[7, Lemma 6.2(b)(3) and its proof]` plus `[7, Definition 1.11]`
  is enough to conclude the descendant-with-elliptic-boundary statement on the
  packet alone.
- Two primaries treated Proposition `9.65` as `unclear` because the final step
  also uses an uncited contraction of the disjoint remainder `D_rest` and an
  external descendant definition that is not unpacked on these pages. One
  primary treated the citation as precise enough and marked the proposition
  `verified`. Without usable `unsure` adjudications, the conservative merged
  classification is `unclear`.
- Proposition `9.68` is stable across all reviews. The ruling from Proposition
  `9.56`, the verticality of all later blowups over `A^2`, and the final
  intersection count `F · D_Z = 1` all check.
