## Normalized Claim

Proposition `9.71` claims that the full peelable family
`[2,2] ⊣ [3,(2)^m]` already carries a ruling whose general fiber meets the
reduced exceptional divisor once, after contracting the first three local
curves.

Corollary `9.72` then concludes that any surface carrying that family has
`ht(X) = 1`, so the family cannot survive in the residual height-`>= 4`,
no-descendant sector.

Corollary `9.73` packages the first-frontier reduction and says that, assuming
the boxed Route C entry statement and frontier completeness, only the two
higher-square tail prototypes remain.

Proposition `9.75` claims that the two higher-square short bridges
`[2] ⊣ [3,2,2]` and `[2] ⊣ [4,2,2,2]` already carry rulings whose general
fiber meets the reduced exceptional divisor twice.

Corollary `9.76` then concludes that either short bridge forces `ht(X) <= 2`,
so neither can survive in the residual height-`>= 4` sector.

## Agreed Findings

- Proposition `9.71` is locally sound. The contraction arithmetic
  `L, U_1, E_1` is correct, the resulting chain
  `F(0)-S(-1)-E_3(-2)-...-E_n(-2)` is correct, and the base-point-free pencil
  argument does produce a morphism `f : Y -> P^1`.
- The focused `unsure` reviewers agreed that the objection
  “base-point-free pencil does not yield a real ruling/general fiber” is
  spurious in Proposition `9.71`. The statement itself already assumes that
  every other connected component of `D` is disjoint from the peelable bridge,
  which is exactly the global isolation needed for the verticality and lifting
  arguments.
- Proposition `9.71` still uses the rationality of `Y` and the vanishing
  `H^1(Y, O_Y) = 0` without citation, but this was adjudicated as a
  citation/clarity issue rather than a packet-level proof failure.
- Corollary `9.72` therefore verifies as a short deduction from Proposition
  `9.71`, Lemma `9.3`, and Theorem `9.1`.
- Corollary `9.73` is structurally coherent, but its packet-level status is
  weaker than “verified”. The proof does eliminate the peelable family and the
  two fork bridges by citing Corollaries `9.72` and `9.66`, but it relies on
  imported upstream inputs and does not fully unpack the broader reduction
  statement on these pages. The conflict reviews resolved this as `unclear`,
  not as a clean `gap`.
- Proposition `9.75` has a real packet-level gap. The local contraction
  arithmetic is correct, and the `unsure` reviewers agreed that the
  pencil-to-ruling objection is again spurious. But unlike Proposition `9.71`,
  Proposition `9.75` does not assume, and pages `73-77` do not prove, the
  global isolation fact that every component of `D` outside the displayed
  bridge is disjoint from `U`, `L`, and `E_2`.
- That missing global-isolation input is used twice in Proposition `9.75`:
  first to conclude that every other component of `σ_*D` is vertical, and then
  to place the first inverse blowup center on `F` away from all other
  components.
- Corollary `9.76` inherits the Proposition `9.75` gap, because its height
  bound uses the ruling claimed there.
- Remark `9.74` is a roadmap summary, not a proof item.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Proposition `9.71` uses rationality/vanishing without citation.
- Corollaries `9.72` and `9.76` use the earlier definition of height without
  restating it.
- Corollary `9.73` treats the cited upstream reductions as imported black
  boxes; under a stricter packet-bounded standard this leaves it `unclear`.
- Proposition `9.75` needs a global-isolation hypothesis stronger than what is
  written in the proposition itself.

## Merged Verdict

`9K` is merged with a mixed-support finding:

- Proposition `9.71` is `verified`.
- Corollary `9.72` is `verified`.
- Corollary `9.73` is `unclear` under strict packet-bounded review, because it

  global-isolation fact about the rest of `D`.
- Corollary `9.76` inherits that `gap`.
