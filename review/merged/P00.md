# P00 Merged Memo

Packet: `P00`  
Scope: cover, abstract, `Section 1`, `Section 1.1`, and Table 1 context  
PDF pages: `1-8`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same neutral prompt.
All three agreed on the core point:

- `P00` is introductory/background material, not a proof-bearing packet.
- It clearly announces the paper's claimed theorem and proof program.
- It does not, by itself, establish the seven-point theorem or the listed intermediate results.

## Packet Role

- Announces the main claim: every characteristic-3 klt del Pezzo surface of Picard rank `1` has at most seven singular points.
- Explicitly frames this as resolving the cited FrontierMath / Epoch open problem in the negative.
- Explains the distinction between `Method A`, `Method B`, `Method C`, and the paper's `Route C`.
- States that `Sections 1-8` are contextual and that the formal proof begins in `Section 9`.
- States that `Sections 1-8` preserve the numerical and heuristic exploration that led to the final strategy.
- Summarizes the intended proof architecture and records representative search outcomes in Table 1.

## Agreed Findings

- The packet is coherent as a roadmap and is candid about not being the formal proof spine.
- The main theorem, the reduction to the residual sector, and the nine listed intermediate results are announced rather than proved on these pages.
- The packet announces concrete later claims, not only generic milestones, including the bridge formula `-K_X · π(L) = -1 + μ(A,i) + μ(B,j)`, the contact-defect inequality material, and the excess identity / maximal-width closure package.
- Table 1 is contextual rather than self-justifying; its row outcomes are not established locally on page `8`.
- Many technical notions are used before local definition, including `ht(X)`, `descendant with elliptic boundary`, `residual sector`, `bridge`, `packet`, and `maximal width`.
- The packet depends materially on external citations for literature-status claims and classification context.

## Risks To Carry Forward

- Citation risk: the introductory claim that the seven-point bound is "optimal" and that known constructions reach at most seven points depends on external sources rather than in-packet support.
- Citation risk: the claim that the paper resolves the FrontierMath / Epoch open problem is also citation-sensitive and not justified locally in the packet.
- Citation risk: Table 1 and the opening background paragraph use external classification statements and internal search outcomes without local justification.
- Ambiguity risk: Table 1 mixes labels such as `verified`, `numerical`, and `if realized`, so the evidentiary status of rows is not uniform.
- Forward-dependency risk: the packet points sharply to later sections, with explicit-corridor material deferred to `Sections 3-8`, residual reduction to `Section 9`, and maximal-width / final closure to `Sections 11-12`.

## Merged Verdict

`P00` is accepted as a background/roadmap packet.

Acceptance meaning:

- acceptable as exposition and dependency map,
- not acceptable as standalone support for theorem-level mathematical claims,
- later packets must justify every theorem-level announcement made here.
