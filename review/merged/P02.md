## Normalized Claim

If `Y ⊂ P^4` is a smooth complete intersection of two quadrics in characteristic `3` and `g` is a diagonal involution acting freely in codimension `1` on `Y`, then the quotient `X = Y / <g>` has at most `6` singular points. Hence this Method A corridor cannot reach `8` singular points.

## Agreed Findings

- The proof does not justify why codimension-`1` freeness forces the ambient fixed-locus type `P^2 ⊔ P^1` rather than `P^3 ⊔ P^0`.
- The statement that quotient singularities correspond to isolated points of `Y ∩ Fix(g)` is used without local argument or citation.
- The bound of at most `4` points on the fixed `P^2` is asserted without the needed zero-dimensionality / Bezout argument.
- The bound of at most `2` points on the fixed `P^1` is asserted without the needed restricted-quadratic argument and exclusion of `P^1 ⊂ Y`.
- Once those omitted steps are supplied, the final `4 + 2 = 6` count is immediate.

## Merged Verdict

`P02` is merged with a negative support finding:

- the proposition looks mathematically plausible,
- but the in-packet proof is incomplete on a strict review standard,
- so `Section 3.1` cannot currently be treated as fully demonstrated from the text alone.
