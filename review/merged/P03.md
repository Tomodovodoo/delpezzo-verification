# P03 Merged Memo

Packet: `P03`
Scope: `Section 3.2` only
PDF pages: `9-10`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. All three agreed on the core point:

- Proposition `3.2` has a numerically coherent tail once its displayed formula is granted.
- The geometric heart of the argument is not proved in-packet.
- Remark `3.3` is largely asserted rather than demonstrated.

## Normalized Claim

For a "natural extension" of the characteristic-`3` Frobenius graph construction on
`P^1 x P^1` with `C·F = 3a`, the same triple blow-up over `m` fibres, and
`r = 3m - 6a`, the packet claims

`-K_T · ψ^*F = 2 - 3a(1 - 2/r) = 2 - 3a + 2a/(m - 2a)`

and that positivity occurs only for `(a, m) = (1, 3)`, identified with the known
`7`-point case.

## Agreed Findings

- The construction is not self-contained: phrases such as "natural extension,"
  "same triple blow-up process," and the roles of `F`, `F_i`, `S`, and `ψ` are not
  defined locally.
- The key formula `C^2 = 6a - 3m = -r` is asserted without calculation.
- The discrepancy formula is asserted without derivation or precise citation.
- The sentence "intersecting with a general fibre yields the displayed formula"
  references a computation that is not carried out.
- The proof uses `m > 2a` and effectively positivity/integrality assumptions on
  `a, m`, but these are not stated in the proposition.
- Conditional on the displayed formula and `m > 2a`, the final inequality argument
  to `(a, m) = (1, 3)` is numerically sound.
- The sentence "the next step only makes `-K` nef, not ample" overstates what the
  displayed fibre intersection alone proves; failure of ampleness is shown, nefness
  is not.
- The identification of `(a, m) = (1, 3)` with the known `7`-point case is asserted,
  but not demonstrated inside this packet.
- The packet also does not verify existence or admissibility of the claimed general
  `a, m` extension before applying the numerical argument to it.
- Remark `3.3` does not derive its basket counts, smooth blowdown, or
  nef/ampleness conclusions.

## Merged Verdict

`P03` is merged with a negative support finding:

- the final numerical obstruction is plausible,
- but Proposition `3.2` is only partially supported as written,
- and Remark `3.3` is substantially unsupported within the packet.
