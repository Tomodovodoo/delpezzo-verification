# P04 Merged Memo

Packet: `P04`
Scope: `Section 4.1` only
PDF pages: `10-11`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. They agreed on the main picture:

- Proposition `4.1` is proof-bearing, not background-only.
- The local quotient identifications and the displayed `K^2` arithmetic are mostly
  correct at the formal level.
- The packet does not fully justify the exact basket, the rationality step, or the
  exact-`8` corollary under a strict review standard.

## Normalized Claim

For the family

`X^(n)_(a,p,q) = {f_n(x0,x1) + x2 x3 = 0} subset P(a,a,p,q),`

with `p + q = an`, `f_n` split with `n` distinct roots, `gcd(a,p)=gcd(a,q)=1`,
and the printed condition `3 does not divide apqn`, Proposition `4.1` claims:

- the singularity basket is `n A_(a-1) + [p] + [q]`,
- `K_X^2 = 4(p+q)/(pq)`,
- `K^2_(X~) = 8 - (p+q) = 8 - an`,
- `X~` is rational and `rho(X) = n`.

The packet then claims that exact `8` singular points force `n = 6`, and gives
two displayed examples with baskets `6A1 + [5] + [7]` and `6A3 + [5] + [19]`.

## Agreed Findings

- On the singular line `P(a,a)`, the restriction `f_n = 0` does produce `n`
  reduced points if the stated distinct-root hypothesis is read literally.
- The local quotient-type identifications
  `1/a(p,q) ~= 1/a(1,-1) = A_(a-1)` and
  `1/p(a,a) ~= 1/p(1,1)`, `1/q(a,a) ~= 1/q(1,1)`
  are mathematically plausible and were not the point of dispute.
- The displayed weighted-hypersurface computation
  `K_X^2 = 4(p+q)/(pq)` is arithmetically correct, conditional on applicability
  of the cited formula.
- The displayed simplification
  `K^2_(X~) = 8 - (p+q)` is also arithmetically correct, conditional on the usual
  crepantness of `A_(a-1)` and the standard `1/r(1,1)` drop formula.
- The proof does not explicitly establish that the listed singularities are all
  the singularities of `X`; it leans on the earlier sentence that the family is
  tame and quasi-smooth, which is asserted rather than proved here.
- One reviewer noted that the exclusion of extra smooth-locus singularities is
  recoverable from the equation itself using `F_x2 = x3` and `F_x3 = x2`, but the
  packet does not present that argument.
- The rationality step is compressed to "the equation is linear in `x3` on the
  chart `x2 != 0`," which is plausible but not fully written out in the packet.
- The Picard-number computation
  `rho(X) = rho(X~) - (n(a-1) + 2) = n`
  assumes that the two coordinate-point terms really are singularities and that
  the exceptional divisor has exactly `n(a-1) + 2` components.
- The exact-`8` deduction `n + 2 = 8` silently requires the total singular-point
  count really to be `n + 2`, with no extra singularities and no smooth placeholders.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- The packet silently needs `a > 1`, `p > 1`, and `q > 1` if the symbols
  `A_(a-1)`, `[p]`, and `[q]` are meant to denote actual singular points and if
  the singular-point count is meant literally, rather than allowing smooth cases
  such as `A_0` or `[1]`.
- The claim that the family is tame and quasi-smooth is used but not proved or
  precisely cited inside the packet.
- The weighted-hypersurface `K^2` formula, the `1/r(1,1)` contribution formula,
  and the Picard-number subtraction across resolution are invoked only generically.
- The displayed examples are not independently checked against all family
  hypotheses inside this subsection.

## Discrepancy Resolution

Two of the three primaries flagged an additional issue: the printed family
condition `3 does not divide apqn` literally excludes `n = 6`, while the packet
immediately derives exact `8` singular points imply `n = 6` and then presents two
`n = 6` examples.

This was escalated to two neutral `unsure` agents. Both agreed on:

- there is a real literal inconsistency in the printed wording,
- no ordinary reading of `3 does not divide apqn` allows the displayed `n = 6`
  examples,
- but the local context strongly suggests this is a localized notation/slip issue,
  not a substantive mathematical obstruction.

Strictly for review purposes, the packet is inconsistent as written on this point.

## Merged Verdict

`P04` is merged with a negative support finding:

- the local quotient identifications and the displayed numerical formulas are mostly
  credible,
- but Proposition `4.1` is not fully justified as written,
- the exact-`8` corollary depends on unstated weight and exhaustiveness assumptions,
- and the printed condition `3 does not divide apqn` is textually inconsistent with
  the later `n = 6` discussion, most likely because of a local slip.
