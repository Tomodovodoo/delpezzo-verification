# P05 Merged Memo

Packet: `P05`
Scope: `Section 4.2` only
PDF pages: `11-12`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. All three agreed on the core point:

- Proposition `4.2` is not background-only.
- Its numerical `K^2` and Picard-number algebra are coherent once the intended
  singularity pattern is granted.
- The proof does not establish that singularity pattern or the rationality step
  with enough precision for strict acceptance.

## Normalized Claim

For the symmetric family

`X_ab subset P(a,a,b,b), F = f_b(x0,x1) + g_a(x2,x3),`

with `gcd(a,b) = 1` and `3 does not divide ab`, Proposition `4.2` claims

`rho(X) = (a - 1)(b - 1) + 1,`

and concludes that large singular baskets in this family automatically force
large Picard number.

The packet also gives the concrete example

`x0^7 + x1^7 + x2^2 + x3^2 = 0 subset P(2,2,7,7),`

claimed to have `9` singular points and `rho = 7`.

## Agreed Findings

- The proof skeleton is clear: count singularities, compute `K_X^2`, deduce
  `K^2_(X~)`, assert rationality, compute `rho(X~)`, and subtract exceptional
  components.
- The displayed weighted-hypersurface calculation
  `K_X^2 = (2a + 2b - ab)^2 / (ab)` is arithmetically correct if the standard
  formula applies.
- The displayed simplification `K^2_(X~) = 8 - ab` is also arithmetically correct
  if one truly has exactly `b` points of type `1/a(1,1)` and `a` points of type
  `1/b(1,1)`.
- The proof does not justify those exact singularity counts for the whole stated
  family. They require extra input such as squarefreeness or transversality of
  `f_b` and `g_a`, and also the absence of any additional singularities.
- The proof does not precisely justify the sentence "As before the surface is
  rational," even though rationality is then used to obtain `rho(X~) = ab + 2`.
- The exceptional-divisor count `a + b` is only correct when `a, b > 1` and when
  the singularities really are exactly the stated `1/a(1,1)` and `1/b(1,1)` points.
- All three reviewers treated the concrete `(a,b) = (2,7)` example as locally
  consistent with the intended argument, provided geometric singular points are
  being counted over an algebraically closed field.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- The packet needs unstated squarefreeness or separability assumptions on `f_b`
  and `g_a` if it wants exact counts of `b` and `a` singular points.
- It also needs a no-extra-singularities argument or a quasi-smoothness-type input.
- The packet silently needs `a > 1` and `b > 1` if the singularity and exceptional
  divisor counts are meant literally, even though some displayed algebraic
  identities still make formal sense outside that range.
- The weighted-hypersurface `K^2` formula, the `1/r(1,1)` resolution drop, and
  the rational-surface Picard formula are invoked without precise citations or
  applicability checks.
- Exact singular-point counts are implicitly geometric counts; the packet does not
  restate the base-field convention here.

## Merged Verdict

`P05` is merged with a negative support finding:

- the numerical core of Proposition `4.2` is conditionally correct,
- but the packet does not prove the exact basket or the especially compressed
  rationality input needed
  for the displayed Picard-number formula,
- so under a strict standard the proposition remains only partially supported as written.
