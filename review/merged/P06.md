# P06 Merged Memo

Packet: `P06`
Scope: `Section 5` only
PDF pages: `12-13`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. They agreed on the main picture:

- Section `5` is proof-bearing, not background-only.
- Proposition `5.1` presents a plausible obstruction strategy, but only as a sketch.
- The sketch relies on major uncited geometric/combinatorial inputs and does not
  fully derive its headline inequality inside the packet.
- Remark `5.2` is a stronger special-case assertion with no proof given here.

## Normalized Claim

Proposition `5.1` considers an exact-`8` member

`X = X^(6)_(a,p,q) = {f6(x0,x1) + x2 x3 = 0} subset P(a,a,p,q), p + q = 6a,`

with minimal resolution `X~`. It then studies any birational process obtained by
smooth blowdowns on `X~` followed by contraction of the remaining negative-definite
divisor to a klt surface `Y`.

The proposition claims:

- if `r` measures the surviving contribution from the six `A_(a-1)` branches, then
  `rho(Y) >= r`,
- hence `rho(Y) = 1` forces at most one surviving branch,
- therefore `#Sing(Y) <= 3`,
- so no exact-`8` member of this family can be birationally reduced to Picard
  number `1` while retaining `8` singular points.

Remark `5.2` then claims a stronger special-case conclusion for the smallest source
`P(2,2,5,7)`: any Picard-rank-`1` output has at most one singular point.

## Agreed Findings

- The packet depends materially on Section `4`, especially on the exact-`8`
  interpretation of `X^(6)_(a,p,q)` and the value `rho(X~) = 6a + 2`.
- All three reviewers agreed that the visible graph used in the sketch
  (`E_p`, `E_q`, and six branches `V_i - A_(i,a-1) - ... - A_(i,1) - C_i`)
  is not proved or precisely cited inside Section `5`.
- All three reviewers agreed that the sentence
  "A separate intersection argument shows that every non-visible irreducible curve
  has nonnegative self-intersection"
  is a critical but unusable citation: the contraction game is reduced to the visible
  graph without a stated lemma or reference.
- All three reviewers agreed that the final inequality `rho(Y) >= r` is not actually
  derived in the packet text. The sketch only gestures at upper bounds on the number
  of smooth blowdowns `t` and the number of final exceptional components `m`.
- One reviewer reconstructed the intended arithmetic:
  if the branchwise bounds are granted and `r` is interpreted as the number of
  surviving branches, then the inequality `rho(Y) >= r` is numerically plausible.
- All three reviewers agreed that the step from `rho(Y) = 1` to `#Sing(Y) <= 3`
  remains only sketched, because the packet does not justify exactly how many
  singular points can survive from the remaining branches and endpoints.
- All three reviewers agreed that Remark `5.2` is assertion only in this packet.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- The wording of Proposition `5.1` is ambiguous: the statement says `r` is the
  number of surviving branch components, while the proof uses `r` as though it
  were the number of surviving branches.
- Section `5` imports from Proposition `4.1` more than just notation:
  it uses the exact-`8` family setup and the resolution numerics, and although it
  does restate the visible graph, it does not justify or precisely cite that geometry.
- Read literally, the Section `4.1` condition `3 does not divide apqn` excludes the
  exact-`8` case `n = 6` that Section `5` then analyzes. The Section `4` review
  treated this as a likely localized slip rather than a substantive exclusion, but
  Section `5` inherits the inconsistency at the textual level.
- The passage `rho(Y) = rho(X~) - t - m` is used without explanation of the exact
  bookkeeping assumptions behind `t` and `m`.
- Terms such as "branch survives," "branch dies completely," and "surviving branch
  components" are not formally defined.

## Discrepancy Resolution

One primary reviewer flagged a specific flaw in the sketch's local branchwise bound:

- for a surviving branch, the sketch says the number of smooth blowdowns spent on
  that branch is at most `(a - 1) - s_i`,
- but for `a = 2`, the displayed branch is `V_i - A_(i,1) - C_i`,
- and if `A_(i,1)` survives, one can still blow down `V_i`, giving one smooth
  blowdown even though `(a - 1) - s_i = 0`.

This conflict was escalated to two neutral `unsure` agents. Both agreed on:

- the `a = 2` example exposes a real flaw in the branchwise bound as written,
- the literal local accounting sentence is therefore defective,
- but the packet does not rule out that the author intended a different bookkeeping
  convention in which outer-curve blowdowns are charged elsewhere,
- so this local flaw does not by itself settle whether the global conclusion of
  Proposition `5.1` is false.

Strictly for review purposes, the sketch contains a genuine defect in this local bound.

## Merged Verdict

`P06` is merged with a negative support finding:

- Proposition `5.1` outlines a plausible obstruction strategy,
- but its visible-graph reduction, its key combinatorial inputs, the precise meaning
  of `r`, and the singularity-count conclusion are not proved inside the packet,
- and one local branchwise blowdown bound is literally flawed as written.

Remark `5.2` remains unclear because it is asserted without proof or precise citation.
