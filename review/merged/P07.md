# P07 Merged Memo

Packet: `P07`
Scope: `Sections 6.1-6.2` only
PDF page: `13`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. They agreed on the main picture:

- Proposition `6.1` is only sketched and is not proved in-packet.
- Proposition `6.2` contains a genuine local Jacobian argument that is largely
  correct, modulo standard ambient conventions.

## Normalized Claim

Proposition `6.1` claims that in the family

`X_(d1,d2) subset P(a,a,b,b,c),`

if the two repeated singular lines are cut in the separated-strata way and the
construction produces exactly `8` singular points, then one line multiplicity
must be `1`, hence one equation is linear on a repeated-weight block and the
model reduces to a hypersurface.

Proposition `6.2` claims that if

`X_(d1,d2) subset P(a,a,b,b,c), gcd(a,b)=gcd(a,c)=1,`

and both restrictions to the singular line `P(a,a)` are nonzero and share a
nontrivial common factor, then `X` is not quasismooth.

## Agreed Findings

- For Proposition `6.1`, the packet supplies only a high-level sketch:
  weighted Bezout counts on repeated lines plus a Fano index inequality are named,
  but no formulas or case analysis are written out.
- The packet does not derive the key implications
  "line multiplicity `1` implies linearity in a repeated-weight block" and
  "linearity implies hypersurface reduction."
- Terms such as "separated-strata way," "line multiplicity," and "reduces to a
  hypersurface" are used without local definition or precise citation.
- For Proposition `6.2`, all three reviewers agreed that the local proof is
  mathematically sound at the packet level:
  factor the restrictions as `G h_1` and `G h_2`, choose a root of `G` on `P(a,a)`,
  note the tangent derivatives are proportional there, and show the normal
  derivatives vanish because no degree-`d_i` monomial can be linear in
  `x2`, `x3`, or `x4`.
- The inference `a | d_i` from nonzero restriction to `P(a,a)` is implicit but correct.
- The final prose sentence in Section `6.2` is best read as a restatement of the
  proposition within the same coprime quasismooth setting, rather than as a
  substantially broader claim.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Proposition `6.1` invokes weighted Bezout counts and a Fano index inequality
  without any precise formula or citation.
- Proposition `6.2` uses standard but unstated ambient conventions:
  the usual Jacobian criterion for quasismoothness,
  the existence of a root of the nontrivial factor `G` after passing to an
  algebraically closed field,
  and effectively `a > 1` when interpreting `P(a,a)` as the relevant singular line.

## Merged Verdict

`P07` is merged with a mixed finding:

- Proposition `6.1` is a gap: it is only a sketch and does not prove its exclusion claim.
- Proposition `6.2` is verified modulo standard implicit conventions about the
  base field and the Jacobian criterion for quasismoothness.
