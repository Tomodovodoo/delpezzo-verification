# P08 Merged Memo

Packet: `P08`
Scope: `Sections 6.3-6.5` only
PDF pages: `14-15`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. All three agreed on the overall point:

- Propositions `6.3`, `6.4`, and `6.5` are all presented only as idea-level
  exclusion strategies.
- None of the three propositions is proved in-packet under a strict standard.

## Normalized Claim

Proposition `6.3` claims that there is no tame quasismooth del Pezzo complete
intersection

`X_(d1,d2) subset P(a,a,a,b,b)`

with exactly `8` singular points, except in hypersurface-reduction cases where
one equation is linear in the `(x0,x1,x2)` block.

Proposition `6.4` claims that there is no tame quasismooth del Pezzo complete
intersection

`X_(d1,d2) subset P(a,a,ma,b,b)`

with exactly `8` singular points.

Proposition `6.5` claims that in the family

`X_(d1,d2) subset P(a,a,ma,b,c), gcd(b,c) > 1, 3 does not divide abcm,`

the standard reduced-plane mechanism and the simple-vertex plane mechanism
cannot produce exactly `8` singular points.

The closing paragraph on the coprime case `gcd(b,c)=1` is best read as a separate
numerical-search report, not as a proved theorem.

## Agreed Findings

- Proposition `6.3` relies on asserted counts from the singular plane and repeated
  line, but neither the precise counting formulas nor the exact-`8` case split are
  derived in the packet.
- The packet does not justify why the case "both degrees are multiples of `a`"
  captures the non-hypersurface situation before invoking the exclusion argument.
- Proposition `6.4` uses a local intersection count at the weighted-plane vertex
  without showing that local calculation.
- The preliminary forcing `d_i = r_i a` and `r_i = 1 (mod m)` at the weighted-plane
  vertex is also asserted rather than demonstrated on the page.
- The reduction from that count to finitely many arithmetic possibilities is not
  documented, and the final rejection of `X_(10,10) subset P(2,2,8,5,5)` is asserted
  without a local quasismoothness check.
- Proposition `6.5` contains one semigroup-style lower-bound step that is plausible
  under extra assumptions, but the decisive incompatibility with the named plane
  mechanisms is not shown in-packet.
- The final paragraph for the coprime case is empirical search reporting, not a proof.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Across all three propositions, crucial tools are invoked only by description:
  Fano index inequality, divisibility constraints for line points, local intersection
  calculation, reduced weighted-plane Bezout number, simple-vertex count, and related
  mechanism names.
- The packet repeatedly moves from intersection-style quantities to counts of
  singular points without stating reducedness, transversality, or distinctness hypotheses.
- Proposition `6.5` uses side assumptions such as `b,c >= ma + 1` and coprimality
  with `a` inside the idea, but these are not all present in the proposition statement.

## Discrepancy Resolution

One narrow conflict arose around the displayed count in Section `6.4`.
From plain-text extraction alone, the formula looked ambiguous between

- `r_1 r_2 / m`, and
- `1 + (r_1 r_2 - 1) / m`.

This was escalated to two neutral `unsure` agents. Both agreed on:

- the plain-text extraction is genuinely ambiguous by itself,
- but the PDF page strongly favors the reading
  `1 + (r_1 r_2 - 1) / m`,
- and that reading is also the one compatible with the surrounding prose and the
  later candidate arithmetic,
- yet the packet still does not justify the local count, because it merely says
  a local intersection calculation shows it.

So the merged review does not treat Section `6.4` as numerically inconsistent in
the stronger sense; instead it treats the formula as likely readable on the page
but still unsupported in the packet.

## Merged Verdict

`P08` is merged with a negative support finding:

- Proposition `6.3` is a gap,
- Proposition `6.4` is a gap,
- Proposition `6.5` is a gap,
- and the closing coprime-case paragraph is best treated as a search report rather
  than as a proved exclusion theorem.
