# P09 Merged Memo

Packet: `P09`
Scope: `Sections 7.1-7.3` only
PDF pages: `15-18`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. They agreed on the main picture:

- Proposition `7.1` and Corollary `7.2` contain coherent Euler-characteristic
  manipulations, but their key cohomological inputs are only asserted.
- Proposition `7.3` contains a real elimination/unprojection calculation that works
  algebraically, but its stronger singularity-count conclusion is not established
  in-packet.

## Normalized Claim

Proposition `7.1` claims that if `Y` is a smooth rational surface, `G` is a finite
group of order prime to `3` acting freely in codimension `1`, `X = Y/G`, and
`rho(X) = 1`, then `#Sing(X) <= 5`.

Corollary `7.2` claims that if `Y` is a rational klt surface with exactly `8`
singular points and Picard number `r`, and a nontrivial tame group `G` acts freely
in codimension `1`, then for `X = Y/G` with `rho(X) = 1`,

`#Sing(X) <= 6 - 2(r - 2)/|G|,`

and in particular the earlier exact-`8` weighted hypersurface sources with `r = 6`
again give at most `5` singular points downstairs.

Proposition `7.3` claims that a branch-unprojection step applied to

`X^(6)_(a,p,q) = {f6(x0,x1) + x2x3 = 0} subset P(a,a,p,q)`

eliminates back to the same family with one fewer line root, namely
`X^(5)_(a,p-a,q)` or `X^(5)_(a,p,q-a)`, so every such step lowers the singularity
count by at least one; it also claims that in characteristic `3`, alternating the
two sides of the ladder immediately introduces a weight divisible by `3`.

## Agreed Findings

- For Proposition `7.1`, all three reviewers accepted the Euler-characteristic
  identity after removing the nonfree locus and the final inequality
  `s < 6`, once the packet's asserted cohomological input is granted.
- The weak point in Proposition `7.1` is the compressed sentence
  `rho(X) = 1 therefore e(X) = 3`.
  The packet invokes tame invariant cohomology and algebraicity of degree-`2`
  cohomology, but it does not fully justify the identification needed to turn
  `rho(X) = 1` into `b_2(X) = 1` and then `e(X) = 3`.
- For Corollary `7.2`, the orbit-counting and algebraic rearrangement are again
  correct once the packet's asserted cohomological inputs are granted.
- The weak points in Corollary `7.2` are the same cohomological compression as in
  Proposition `7.1`, together with the uncited formula `e(Y) = 2 + r` for rational
  klt surfaces.
- For Proposition `7.3`, all three reviewers agreed that the explicit graph/elimination
  calculation is supported:
  define `s = x2/l = -g5/x3` on the complement of the stated divisor, write the graph
  equations, eliminate `x2`, and obtain the hypersurface of the same family with one
  fewer line root.
- The mod-`3` residue calculation at the end of Proposition `7.3` is also correct.
- The unsupported step in Proposition `7.3` is the jump from "one root of `f6` is removed"
  to "the total singularity count drops by at least one."
  The packet does not show here that no new singularities appear elsewhere, nor does it
  re-establish all of the earlier singularity-description input it uses.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- No precise citation is given in Section `7` for the tame invariant-cohomology
  statement, the algebraicity/vanishing input needed to compute Euler characteristics,
  or the formula `e(Y) = 2 + r` for rational klt surfaces.
- Proposition `7.3` uses several earlier conventions and inputs that are not
  re-established in this packet:
  the notation `X^(5)`,
  the carried-over claim that the repeated line contributes six singular points in
  the degree-`6a` family,
  and the convention that a weight divisible by `3` leaves the tame characteristic-`3` setting.
- Proposition `7.3` also uses unstated hypotheses that the factorization `f6 = l g5`
  exists and that the shifted weights `p-a` and `q-a` are positive.
- One reviewer noted that the phrase "codimension-2 divisor" is imprecise for the
  locus `(l = x3 = 0)` on a surface; this is a wording issue, not the main source
  of the packet's mathematical weakness.

## Merged Verdict

`P09` is merged with a mixed negative-support finding:

- Proposition `7.1` is unclear: its Euler-characteristic argument is coherent, but
  its key cohomological step is asserted rather than established in-packet.
- Corollary `7.2` is unclear for the same reason.
- Proposition `7.3` is a gap: the elimination and mod-`3` arithmetic are supported,
  but the claimed drop in total singularity count, and hence the final exclusion
  conclusion, is not proved from what is written here.
