# P10 Merged Memo

Packet: `P10`
Scope: `Sections 8.1-8.5` only
PDF pages: `18-20`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. They agreed on the main structure:

- `Sections 8.1`, `8.3`, and `8.4`, plus the search-output part of `8.5`, are
  mostly background, heuristic motivation, or reported computation.
- The only formal proof-bearing item in the packet is Proposition `8.1`.
- A short proof-like arithmetic claim appears at the end of `8.2`, but its
  geometric conclusion is not justified in-packet.

Because one primary treated Proposition `8.1` as verified while two marked it
unclear, the conflict was escalated to two neutral `unsure` agents. Both agreed
that the discrepancy arithmetic is correct, but the packet-level verdict should
still be `unclear`, not `verified`, because the final Picard-number step uses a
standard but uncited rational-surface input not established within `P10`.

## Normalized Claim

Section `8.1` reports a published codimension-`4` exact-`8` example from
Qureshi and states that, after resolving its basket, one finds

`K^2_(tilde X) = -7, rho(tilde X) = 17, rho(X) = 8,`

so it does not solve the rank-`1` problem.

Section `8.2` reconstructs the corresponding row-`6` arithmetic pattern and
claims that the direct analogue can never be tame in characteristic `3`, because
the ambient pattern has three consecutive step-`2` weights `d, d+2, d+4`.

Section `8.3` offers GLSM/orbifold-CFT motivation for a spectrum-first search.

Section `8.4` presents the weighted-cube Hilbert-series model and the local
orbifold correction term used in the search, together with the claim that the
extra local class parameter `c` is needed to recover the published exact-`8`
row.

Section `8.5` reports a current exact tame `8`-sector search hit with ambient
space `P(1,2,4,4,5,5,7)`, basket `5 x [4] + 2 x [3,2] + [7]`, and
`K_X^2 = 13/35`, and Proposition `8.1` claims that any klt del Pezzo surface
with exactly those numerical invariants must satisfy `rho(X) = 9`.

## Agreed Findings

- The packet does not establish the reported search outputs in `8.4-8.5` as
  mathematical results. These are best read as solver inputs and solver reports,
  not as proved existence statements.
- Section `8.1` also gives an immediate characteristic-`3` obstruction for the
  published Qureshi row: its basket contains `1/3` singularities, so it is not
  a tame characteristic-`3` candidate even before the Picard-number computation.
- In `8.1`, the numerical Picard-number rejection of the published Qureshi row
  is arithmetically consistent, but the computation is only asserted rather than
  proved on the page.
- In `8.2`, the modular arithmetic is correct: among `d, d+2, d+4`, one is
  divisible by `3`.
- The unsupported step in `8.2` is the jump from that divisibility observation
  to the geometric claim that the resulting model cannot be tame in
  characteristic `3`.
- Proposition `8.1` has correct discrepancy arithmetic:
  `[4]` contributes `1`, `[3,2]` contributes `2/5`, `[7]` contributes `25/7`,
  so

  `K_X^2 - K^2_(tilde X) = 5 x 1 + 2 x 2/5 + 25/7 = 328/35,`

  hence `K^2_(tilde X) = -9`, and the exceptional divisor has `10` irreducible
  components.
- The last step of Proposition `8.1`,

  `rho(tilde X) = 10 - K^2_(tilde X) = 19` and `rho(X) = rho(tilde X) - 10 = 9`,

  is conditionally correct if one imports standard external facts:
  `tilde X` is a smooth rational surface, `rho(S) = 10 - K_S^2` for smooth
  rational surfaces, and contracting the exceptional configuration lowers Picard
  number by the number of irreducible exceptional components.
- Those facts are not proved or cited within `P10`, so the packet does not by
  itself justify the full Proposition `8.1` conclusion under the strict review
  standard used in this project.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- Citation `[11]` identifies Qureshi's paper but is not a pinpoint citation for
  the exact row used in `8.1`.
- The packet does not cite the discrepancy formulas or the rational-surface
  Picard-number formula used implicitly in both the `8.1` computation and
  Proposition `8.1`.
- In `8.2`, the model-to-tameness principle is left unstated.
- In `8.4`, the Hilbert-series model, the local correction term `delta_(r,a,c)`,
  and the role of the extra class parameter `c` are described as search inputs,
  but are not derived or justified in the packet.
- The packet does not provide enough reproducibility detail to independently
  verify the solver outputs reported in `8.4-8.5`.

## Discrepancy Resolution

One substantial conflict arose on the verdict for Proposition `8.1`.

- One primary reviewer treated the proposition as verified because the local
  arithmetic checks out.
- Two primary reviewers marked it unclear because the final Picard-number step
  depends on an uncited standard theorem outside the packet.

This was escalated to two neutral `unsure` agents. Both agreed that:

- the arithmetic in Proposition `8.1` is verified,
- the conclusion `rho(X) = 9` is conditionally correct given standard outside
  input,
- but the missing rational-surface/Picard-number bridge is a real packet-level
  omission, not merely a stylistic citation issue,
- so the correct packet-level verdict is `unclear`, not `verified` and not
  `gap`.

## Merged Verdict

`P10` is merged with a mixed negative-support finding:

- the `8.1` Picard-number rejection of the published example is arithmetically
  correct but only asserted in-packet,
- the final claim of `8.2` is a gap because the modular arithmetic does not by
  itself prove non-tameness,
- `Sections 8.3-8.4` and the search-output part of `8.5` are background,
  heuristic, or reported computation rather than proof,
- and Proposition `8.1` is `unclear`: its discrepancy arithmetic is verified,
  but its Picard-number conclusion depends on a standard external input not
  established or cited within the packet.
