# P11 Merged Memo

Packet: `P11`
Scope: `Sections 8.6-8.8` only
PDF pages: `20-23`

## Consensus

Three fresh, context-free primary reviewers assessed this packet under the same
neutral prompt. All three agreed on the overall picture:

- `Sections 8.6-8.8` are computation-driven elimination/reporting, not
  self-contained deductive proof.
- The packet's bridge from basket data to Picard number,

  `rho_num := 10 - K_X^2 + sum A(sigma) - sum l(sigma),`

  is asserted rather than proved or cited in scope.
- Proposition `8.3` contains a stronger internal defect than `8.2` or `8.4`,
  because its exact-MILP exclusion on the unresolved fringe imposes an
  empirically observed row-index identity not proved necessary in the packet.

## Normalized Claim

Section `8.6` introduces the numerical Picard-rank predictor `rho_num` and then
claims that, in the no-cone, one-section, pair-max-`3` weighted-cube window,
there are exactly `590` admissible rows, `15` exact tame `8`-sector
decompositions, no decomposition with integral `rho_num = 1`, and minimum
integral `rho_num = 2` (Proposition `8.2`).

Section `8.7` claims that, in the enlarged no-cone, one-section, pair-max-`4`
window, there are exactly `2207` admissible rows, exactly `37` exact tame
`8`-sector decompositions, none with integral `rho_num = 1`, minimum integral
`rho_num = 2`, and no new exact hit in the previously unresolved `298`-row
high-basis fringe (Proposition `8.3`).

Section `8.8` claims that, in the first one-cone extension, there are exactly
`5247` admissible rows, `84` exact tame `8`-sector decompositions, none with
integral `rho_num = 1`, minimum integral `rho_num = 2`, and that the previously
seen ambient space `P(1,2,4,4,5,5,7)` reappears only with minimum integral
value `rho_num = 9` (Proposition `8.4`).

## Agreed Findings

- The opening `rho_num` principle in `8.6` is not derived in the packet. It is
  compatible with the standard resolution/Picard formulas, but those formulas
  are neither proved nor cited here.
- Propositions `8.2-8.4` are presented as outputs of exact computational scans,
  not as self-contained proofs.
- In Propositions `8.2` and `8.4`, the paper searches only decompositions whose
  local indices divide at least one final ambient weight, but the packet does
  not justify that this restriction is complete for the stated propositions.
- The counts `590`, `15`, `2207`, `37`, `298`, `191`, `5247`, and `84`, as well
  as the reported `rho_num` values for the current row, are not reproducible
  from the text alone.
- A wording tension appears in `8.6` and `8.8`: rows are said to be kept or
  retained only if a decomposition is compatible with `rho_num = 1`, yet the
  subsequent computation paragraphs report many exact decompositions with
  `rho_num != 1`. The intended search/filter protocol is therefore not fully
  clear from the prose.
- Proposition `8.3` goes beyond simple search reporting: it imposes the row
  index

  `I = (a1+a2) + (b1+b2) + (c1+c2) - d`

  on the unresolved fringe because it was observed on the `37` previously known
  exact hits. The packet does not prove that every exact hit in the fringe must
  satisfy that identity.
- Consequently, the `8.7` MILP infeasibility result only excludes decompositions
  satisfying the imposed row-index constraint, not necessarily all exact tame
  `8`-sector decompositions in the fringe.

## Hidden Assumptions and Citation Issues

- No external literature citations appear in `P11`.
- Several objects are used without definition in the packet itself:
  `A(sigma)`, `l(sigma)`, weighted-cube Hilbert series, exact tame `8`-sector
  decomposition, tame index set, local basis of correction terms, and
  basis-size.
- The packet repeatedly invokes earlier computational checkpoints and a previous
  grouped exact solver without precise internal citation.
- The search space is described at a high level, but the packet does not provide
  enough detail to reproduce the full decomposition basis, duplicate handling,
  infeasibility certificates, or exact row counts.

## Merged Verdict

`P11` is merged with a negative-support finding:

- the `rho_num` bridge in `8.6` is `unclear` as written,
- Proposition `8.2` is `unclear` because it is a reported exhaustive scan rather
  than a self-contained proof, and because the packet does not justify the
  completeness of its restriction to decompositions whose local indices divide a
  final ambient weight,
- Proposition `8.3` is a `gap` because its negative conclusion over the
  unresolved fringe depends on an empirically observed row-index identity that is
  not proved necessary in-packet,
- and Proposition `8.4` is `unclear` because it too is a reported computational
  exclusion lacking full in-packet verification, full reproducibility detail,
  and a proof that its divisibility-filtered search is complete for the stated
  proposition.
